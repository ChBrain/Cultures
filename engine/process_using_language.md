# Process: Using Language

## The language was chosen before the persona spoke.

## Owner
- Project: Cultures
- Scope: Universal

---

## Initiated by
A persona must move meaning between themselves and the room.
No persona holds only one language, and none holds one relationship to it.
The room chooses which language. The other personas, and the work
that must be done, choose it before the persona decides.

## Direction
Toward the room, or toward the persona.
Four channels carry the meaning, and each runs its own loop.
[Speaking](process_speaking.md) and [Writing](process_writing.md) move it out.
[Hearing](process_hearing.md) and [Reading](process_reading.md) let it in.
The persona does not move through a language evenly.
They move through each channel at the distance that channel allows.

## Lever
The gap between what the persona means and what the language gives them.
Every persona holds a different gap for every language,
and a different gap again for every channel of the same language.
The gap is not skill. It is how far the language sits from the body.
It has four widths: from the language gripped as a foreign object,
to the language that needs no grip because it is already the body.

## Echo
Whether the language held or cracked, recorded per channel.
The gap that opened in one channel does not close in another.
What the persona produced does not vanish. It enters the world
as a thing other personas must now receive.
The loop closes here and opens again
when the next room chooses the next language.

# Position: American

## Owner
- Project: Cultures
- Culture: United States

## Has
The first name. Strangers use it before the second sentence is finished.
The pitch, ready in the elevator and again at the bar.
The deal as the unit of trust, written down, signed, kept in a drawer.
The handshake that closes the deal, then the email that confirms it.
The room as a meritocracy in theory; the senator on the floor is a peer of the bartender on the third shift, on a first-name basis, at least in the rhetoric.

The open field. Anyone can step in. The field does not check the name on the door.
The accessible boss, sleeves rolled up, walking the floor.
The CEO who answers to a first-name address from the intern; not always, but often enough to be approachable.
The second chance, available on application, with a story.
Reinvention as a right, not a favor.
The frontier in the head, even when the land ran out.

The contract. Not a courtesy: a thing you can sue on.
Personal liberty, defended in the courthouse and on the porch.
Self-reliance as the operating mode, not as a slogan on a poster.
Initiative as the default move: you go.

The First Amendment in the schoolroom and on the protest sign.
The right to say it out loud, and the duty to hear it said back.
The public square, paved and noisy, with a stage at one end.

The pursuit of happiness, written into the founding document as a goal of the state.
Not a guarantee. A direction.
Optimism as infrastructure. The can-do as load-bearing, not decoration.
Indulgence on the weekend; the grill, the tailgate, the long weekend at the lake.

## Orders
Say what you do. Say what you did. Say it first.
Show the result, not the effort.
Make the pitch, take the meeting, close the deal.
Carry the ambition openly; be adaptable about how. Winning is the verb in the room.
If it fails, file Chapter 11 and try again.

## Loses
The quiet competence that expected the work to speak.
The patience that waited for someone else to notice.
The room where modesty was read as respect.
The American room reads quiet as nothing, and moves on.

## Drives
On the loss of quiet competence:
The persona learns to narrate. The project, the outcome, the number, the headline.
The persona learns to [pitch](culture_american_process_pitching.md): the elevator version, the one-page version, the deck.
The persona learns that the room moves at the speed of the loudest reasonable voice,
and that velocity, not deliberation, is the American [history](culture_american_piece_history.md) of the deal.

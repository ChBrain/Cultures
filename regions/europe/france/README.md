## How Dimensions Shape This Culture

**PDI High:** Authority exists and is respected - but it must justify itself through competence and argument. The grandes écoles produce a recognized elite. The Republic simultaneously insists on equality. Both are true. Neither cancels the other.

**IDV High:** The individual conscience is the ground of ethical and civic action. The right to form and defend a personal position is not optional - it is demanded. Autonomy is philosophical before it is political.

**MAS Moderate:** France is neither strongly competitive nor strongly caring as a cultural dominant. Dialogue, conciliation, and quality of life carry weight alongside ambition and achievement. The tension is present but not resolved toward either pole.

**UAI Very High:** Uncertainty is managed through rules, procedures, and formal frameworks. The Code civil, the laïcité, the protocol - these are not bureaucratic obstacles; they are the conditions that make the common space legible. Débrouillardise (resourceful improvisation) is the cultural response when the rules fail, not a replacement for them.

**LTO High:** The Republic is a project with continuity. Institutions are built to transmit. The Grande Bibliothèque, the grandes écoles, the Constitution - each is designed to outlast its founders. Patience and perseverance in sustained endeavor are valued.

**IND Moderate:** Restraint and indulgence coexist without strong dominance. Duty and pleasure are both legitimate. The French reputation for joie de vivre is real; so is the tradition of sobriété and abnégation. The balance sits near the midpoint.

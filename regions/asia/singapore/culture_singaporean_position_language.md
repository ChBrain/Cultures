# Language: Singapore English

## Owner
- Project: Cultures
- Culture: Singapore

## Has
The title before the name.
Engineer. Doctor. Manager. Director.
The room does not know what to do with you until it knows your rank.
The title is not courtesy. It is placement.
Without it, the introduction has not happened yet.

Standard register and street register in the same mouth.
Standard Singapore English for the institution, the office, the submission.
Singlish for the coffee shop, the corridor, the message after hours.
Every educated Singaporean holds both and moves between them without announcement.
The switch is not code-switching. It is belonging-switching.

Indirection where relationship has not been established.
A request framed as a question framed as an observation.
The hard truth arrives last, cushioned in context.
Face is not vanity. It is the precondition of continued cooperation.
To embarrass someone in the room is to end the room.

The precise sentence.
Short. Load-bearing. No decoration.
What needs to be said is said. What does not need to be said is not said.
The silence after is not absence. It is the rest of the message.

Lah, lor, leh at the end of a sentence.
Not grammar. Not noise.
Attitude, softened or sharpened, depending on the particle.
These do not translate. They were never meant to.

## Orders
Know your rank and the rank of the person you are addressing before you open your mouth.

## Loses
The ease of the unguarded word.
Singapore English does not have a register for speaking without consequence.
The standard register is always watching.
The Singlish register is always marking.
Every word lands somewhere. The speaker always knows this.
Who is listening is always the first question, even when it is not asked.

## Drives
On that loss:
finds the channel where the message can travel without cost.
The hawker table is that channel.
The WhatsApp group is that channel.
The corridor five minutes before the meeting starts.
The standard register does its work in the room.
The rest of the work happens before the room opens and after it closes.

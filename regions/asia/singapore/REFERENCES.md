## Source Registry

### Hofstede Dimensions
- Hofstede Insights / The Culture Factor Group: primary source for all six dimension scores. URL: hofstede-insights.com. Scores confirmed 2026-05-17.
- Geert Hofstede, Gert Jan Hofstede, Michael Minkov, *Cultures and Organizations: Software of the Mind* (3rd ed., McGraw-Hill, 2010): foundational academic source for dimension definitions and scoring methodology.
- Minkov, M. et al. (2023): large-scale replication study updating IDV and LTO scores. Published via The Culture Factor Group, October 2023.

### Singapore History and Institutions
- National Archives of Singapore (nas.gov.sg): primary source for dates, institutional founding, and official records cited in the history file.
- Ministry of National Development Singapore (mnd.gov.sg): source for HDB, CPF, and urban planning institutional facts.
- Lee Kuan Yew, *From Third World to First: The Singapore Story 1965-2000* (HarperCollins, 2000): primary source for the founding generation's self-account, used to calibrate the LTO-high and PDI-high dimensional signals; and the key source for the bilingual-policy and Speak-Mandarin-Campaign rationale.
- Chua Beng Huat, *Communitarian Ideology and Democracy in Singapore* (Routledge, 1995): academic source for collectivism, community ideology, and the IDV-low dimensional signals.
- UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage List, 2020: source for hawker culture inscription. URL: ich.unesco.org.
- Constitution of the Republic of Singapore, Article 153A: source for the official-languages clause and the Bahasa Kebangsaan designation.

### Singapore English / Singlish
- Leimgruber, J.R.E., *Singapore English: Structure, Variation, and Usage* (Cambridge University Press, 2013): academic source for register variation, Singlish grammar, and the kopi-ordering vocabulary system.
- Wee, Lionel, *Language Without Rights* (Oxford University Press, 2011): source for the bilingual education policy and its institutional framing.

### Singapore Mandarin
- Promote Mandarin Council / Speak Mandarin Campaign archives (mandarin.org.sg): primary source for the 1979 launch and successive annual campaign themes.
- Goh Chok Tong, "Speak Mandarin Campaign" speeches (1991, 1994, 2000): cited via the Singapore Government Press Centre archive (sprinter.gov.sg). The continuity from Lee Kuan Yew's 1979 framing into the second-PM era is documented in these annual addresses.
- Ministry of Education Singapore: bilingual education policy materials and "Mother Tongue Language" curriculum statements (moe.gov.sg).
- Lim, Lisa & Ansaldo, Umberto, *Languages in Contact* (Cambridge University Press, 2016): source for Mandarin-Hokkien-Cantonese substrate effects in Singapore Mandarin.

### Singapore Tamil
- Tamil Murasu (Singapore's Tamil-language daily, first published 1935; now part of SPH Media Trust): cited for Singapore-Tamil register and diaspora-vocabulary documentation.
- Schiffman, Harold F., *A Reference Grammar of Spoken Tamil* (Cambridge University Press, 1999): academic source for Tamil register variation, including formal "வாங்க" / informal "வா" contrast and the diaspora register.
- Saravanan, Vanithamani, "Singapore Tamil" in *Language, Capital, Culture* (NIE Singapore, 2002): source for the Singapore Tamil vs Tamil Nadu Tamil register divergence and the impact of English-medium schooling.
- Ministry of Education Singapore "Mother Tongue Language: Tamil" curriculum: source for the தாய்மொழி policy framing.

### Singapore Malay
- Berita Harian Singapura (Singapore's Malay-language daily, continuously published since 1957): cited for Singapore Malay register and Malay-community civic discourse.
- MUIS (Majlis Ugama Islam Singapura / Islamic Religious Council of Singapore): source for madrasah governance, Friday khutbah register, and the religious-civic Malay register cited in Aisyah's persona.
- Constitution of the Republic of Singapore, Article 153A: the Bahasa Kebangsaan clause and the national-symbolic role of Bahasa Melayu (national anthem, motto, SAF parade commands).
- Asmah Haji Omar, *The Malay Peoples of Malaysia and Their Languages* (Dewan Bahasa dan Pustaka, 1983): comparative source for Malay-Indonesian register divergence (the "boleh" / "bisa" example and related vocabulary differences).
- Mohamed, Noriah, *Sosiolinguistik Bahasa Melayu Standard* (Penerbit USM, 1998): academic source for Bahasa Melayu register variation across formal, religious, and everyday-family settings.

### Kiasu
- Tan, Cheng Han, *Kiasunomics* (World Scientific, 2018): popular academic treatment of kiasu as economic and cultural behaviour in Singapore.
- Ooi, Can-Seng, *Cultural Tourism and Tourism Cultures* (Copenhagen Business School Press, 2002): comparative context for Singapore's cultural self-image.

# Place: Marina Bay

## Owner
- Project: Cultures
- Culture: Singapore

## Shown
Reclaimed land. The bay was water until the 1970s. The skyline that stands on it was not here forty years ago.
The Marina Bay Sands: three towers, a boat-shaped platform on top, visible from everywhere in the city that matters.
Gardens by the Bay: Supertrees lit at night, visible from the expressway.
The Merlion: half-lion, half-fish, water permanently exiting its mouth into the bay it was designed to face.
The Esplanade: two domed structures the city calls the durian, for reasons the city finds funnier than visitors do.
The financial district behind: glass and steel, names of banks readable from the water.
The F1 circuit that runs through the streets here every September.
The National Day Parade route: this is where the country performs itself to itself once a year.
Joggers at six in the morning. The circuit route is a track. People use it as a track.
The bridge that connects the two sides of the bay: pedestrian, permanently open, no toll.

## Holds
The [Singaporean](culture_singaporean_position.md) position at its most constructed.
The [history](culture_singaporean_piece_history.md) of reclamation: the bay that exists here is land that was built.
The [kiasu](culture_singaporean_process_kiasu.md) process at civic scale: a city that prepared the ground before it needed it.
The [hawker centre](culture_singaporean_piece_hawkercentre.md) exists three hundred metres from here, below the towers, unchanged in register.

## Offers
You can stand here and see what the planning looked like at the scale it was intended.
You can watch the city perform its own confidence back at itself - the light show, the leisure of the promenade, the enjoyment, celebration, and entertainment that a city of this scale allows itself in public.
You can walk from the financial district to the garden to the heritage shophouse in fifteen minutes on foot and encounter no seam; at walking speed it feels peer to peer, accessible, no introduction needed.
You can be a tourist here and still accidentally be in the middle of a country performing its own self-image.
You can sit on the steps of the Esplanade at midnight while the city hums with continuity.

## Withheld
The reclamation as cost: the sand came from somewhere. The neighbours from whom it was bought are still neighbours.
What the view from the Marina Bay Sands costs per night and who that cost excludes.
The foreign worker dormitory that is not visible from the Supertrees.
The distance between the National Day Parade on television and the question of who is performing for whom.
The water in the bay, the water in the Straits, the water that still has to be negotiated with Malaysia on a contract that has no permanent resolution.
What the city looks like at six in the morning to the man cleaning the pavement before the joggers arrive.

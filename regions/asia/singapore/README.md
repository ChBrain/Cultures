## Culture Map

Singapore's official multilingualism is four languages and one civic surface. The package mirrors that shape: one English language file (Singapore English -- the institutional working language), and three mother-tongue language files for the three communities the bilingual policy names. Every persona is written in their mother tongue and carries Singapore English as a working ladder at "worn" width; the bilingual reality is performed in the file layout, not just described in it.

The three personas cover the three mother-tongue communities the Constitution names alongside English (Mandarin, Tamil, Malay), and each surfaces a different facet of the same position:

- **Wei Ming (Mandarin)** ── the state institution from inside. The system that built the country, the proceduralist whose Mandarin sits underneath the English memo he is drafting.
- **Priya (Tamil)** ── the private-sector technologist navigating the minority register. The Tamil at home, the Singapore English at standup, the Business English with the regional board.
- **Aisyah (Malay)** ── the religious-civic teacher in the Madrasah system. The Malay that is also Bahasa Kebangsaan, the Arabic embedded in the prose of her subjects, the seam between national-symbolic and daily-religious.

## Persona Trio Architecture

Three personas, three mother tongues, three different relationships to the institution.

**Wei Ming (male, 1963, Mandarin-mother-tongue):** Anchor dimension PDI-high / LTO-high. Built the system from inside. Shadow: the system has become constitutive of him rather than the other way around. He cannot distinguish the consultation that found the real gap from the one that found nothing. He applies the same method to both.

**Priya (female, 1991, Tamil-mother-tongue):** Anchor dimension MAS-high with IDV tension. Shaped by the system, performing the collective so fluently that the performance is no longer distinguishable from belonging. Shadow: the cost is not visible in any standup. It shows up on a Sunday evening in an empty office.

**Aisyah (female, 1978, Malay-mother-tongue):** Anchor dimension IDV-low / IND-low with LTO-high. Religious-civic register inside a state framework. Shadow: she cannot tell the religious question from the life question the religious question is carrying, and she has built her teaching on assuming they are always the same. The students who only wanted a technical answer eventually stop asking.

The three personas sit on different axes of the same six-dimension space. Wei Ming and Priya are an opposing-pole IDV pair (Wei Ming: full IDV-low, circle as infrastructure; Priya: IDV tension, the collective as performance that has started to cost). Aisyah carries the IDV-low collective in its civic-religious form -- a third route through the same low-IDV territory the position file specifies. Three shadows, three distinct failure modes.

## How Dimensions Shape the Package

**PDI -- High:** Hierarchy is present before the conversation begins. Title and seniority precede substance in the language file. The position file carries obedience, authority, and rank as structurally load-bearing. Wei Ming's persona is anchored here: the chain of authority is the frame through which he reads every proposal. Aisyah's persona carries it through the religious-teaching frame: Ustazah-to-student is a steep gradient she does not need to enforce.

**IDV -- Low:** The circle is the primary infrastructure. The position file, the kiasu process, and all three personas carry loyalty, kinship, and in-group as the operating substrate beneath the institution. The hawker centre is the collective in its only non-performed form. The Mandarin, Tamil, and Malay language files each show the in-group register operating below the English working register.

**UAI -- Very Low:** Governance instrumentality is the dominant register. The kiasu process is not anxiety -- it is calibration. The language files carry flexibility and adaptability without framing them as virtues. Rules exist and are followed, but their authority is instrumental.

**MAS -- Moderate:** Achievement and excellence run through every file, anchored in the meritocracy narrative and the kiasu process. Care and welfare appear in the hawker centre and in the community registers of all three personas. Aisyah's care-pole register sits heavily on the wellbeing/balance side. Neither side overwhelms the other.

**LTO -- High:** Perseverance and foresight are embedded in the history file (every entry is a long-term calculation), the position file (continuity, prudence, planning), and Wei Ming's persona (the process that holds because the consultation incorporated what the data missed). Aisyah carries it through inter-generational religious teaching.

**IND -- Moderate:** Restraint and composure in public-facing registers. The hawker centre and the place file both carry the indulgent pole in its designated civic form. Priya's shadow shows the cost of sustained public restraint when the private register has nowhere to go. Aisyah's Friday-afternoon stillness shows the same cost at a different temperature.
